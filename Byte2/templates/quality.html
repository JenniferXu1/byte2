Quality: About This Data Set

The dataset is provided for predicting survival in the Titanic event, given 11 attributes of passengers. There are two columns containing missing data, but one of the column's data can be summarized from another complete column. No unusual distribution of numerical and nominal data is detected.

Is the data complete?

I imported the data into the Trifacta Wrangler and found out attributes of cabin and age contained missing values. The bar charts of the number of missing values for the two attributes are below. For the missing cabin value which describes in which cabin did the passenger live, I do not think it's a concern since the other 'pClass' attribute summarizes the specific cabin information and it is complete; for the missing age values, there might be a problem since the probability of survival can be related to the age of the passenger(i.e young children do not have much experience of saving themselves and swimming).

Is the data coherent?

Attributes are all related to the Titanic event.The nominal attributes such as Sex have coherent data(i.e all are either male or female);Numerical attributes such as Age are also coherent and from the bar chart below, we can tell the range of the age is from 1 to 80. Since Titanic is open to public, I expect the ditributions of age,sex,passenger class are normal and no outlier values. From the pie chart, 64% data are from male and I believe it is not due to the sample bias,probably most crews were male and they were a larger proportion in the original population pool.

Is the data correct?

Since Kaggle did not mention how the dataset was collected, it was hard to trace whether there is a source biase involved in the data collection. To estimate the correctness of the data, I drew the bar chart of the number of survivals and the passenger class/sex,to check any correlations. From the bar chart on the left,larger proportion of first class was survived (50% higher than the lower class). And the bar chart on the right showed that larger proportion of females was survived, which fit the saving rule that "women and children first". In sum, no unusual patterns of data was detected.
