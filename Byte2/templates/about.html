Accountability: About This Data Set

On April 15, 1912, during her maiden voyage, the Titanic sank after colliding with an iceberg, killing 1502 out of 2224 passengers and crew [1]. The dataset gave 891 passengers' 11 attributes(i.e age, sex) and whether they have survived or not.

Where did this data come from?

The dataset is taken from Kaggle, a company that provides a number of datasets for machine learning and data analysis competitions worldwide. And this dataset is the training set provided for participants to build a model predicting the likelihood of survive for a given passenger.On the rightside, the table[2] lists important attributes and their meaning. There is no available version information about the dataset on the website

Who has access to it?

The description of dataset is open to everyone. To download the dataset(i.e csv file), users need to register for members and agree to the terms of privacy and terms of use.However, no users can edit the dataset.
